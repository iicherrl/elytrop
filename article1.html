For centuries, historians and cipher enthusiasts have been trying to decipher the message engraved into the base of the statue of the Fish God in The Fishing Village, Despit. Yesterday, at XX:XX XXX, during the Somnial Equinox, historians at XXXXXX University were able to finally crack the code.

"Now that we've solved it, it's really a simple cipher," states Dr. Merlin Hyfer in an interview with Syndrop News.

What has now been deemed the "Columnar Transcription Cipher" involves writing plaintext out in rows, and then reading out the ciphertext in columns. The order that these columns are read off in depends on the alphabetical order of its keyword.

For example, the ciphered text on the plaque of Piran's statue read "INFIAYSN / HRHBENRT / OOERRDEH / NOTRVATG." When deciphered using the keyword "fish", the message now read "IN HONOR OF THEIR BRAVERY AND STRENGTH."

"It seems like much hasn't changed to this day, which may be comforting to Piran's followers. Hopefully, their followers will take this information to help them better remember the values that they stand for."
